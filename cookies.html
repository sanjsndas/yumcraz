1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your computer or mobile device when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently, as well as to provide reporting information and to personalize your experience. Cookies can be "persistent" or "session" cookies. Persistent cookies remain on your personal computer or mobile device when you go offline, while session cookies are deleted as soon as you close your web browser. They serve various functions, from remembering your login details to tracking your browsing habits across different sites. Essentially, cookies act as a memory for the website, allowing it to recognize your device on subsequent visits and tailor content or services to your preferences. This can lead to a more seamless and enjoyable browsing experience, as the website can recall your settings, items in a shopping cart, or even your preferred language. Without cookies, every time you visited a new page on a website, it would treat you as a completely new visitor, requiring you to re-enter information or re-select preferences repeatedly. This fundamental role makes cookies an integral part of modern web browsing, enabling the dynamic and personalized online environments we've come to expect.

The data stored within cookies is typically non-personally identifiable, meaning it doesn't directly reveal your identity. However, when combined with other information, it can sometimes be used to build a profile of your online activities. This is why understanding cookie types and your control over them is crucial for maintaining your privacy. Cookies are often categorized by their duration (session vs. persistent) and their origin (first-party vs. third-party). Session cookies are temporary and vanish once you close your browser, commonly used for maintaining your login state or shopping cart contents during a single visit. Persistent cookies, on the other hand, remain on your device for a set period or until manually deleted, making them suitable for remembering long-term preferences like language settings or login credentials across multiple visits. Both types play a vital role in optimizing website functionality and user experience, but their distinct lifespans and purposes necessitate different considerations for privacy and data management.

Furthermore, cookies can be classified by their purpose. Essential cookies are fundamental for a website's basic operation, enabling features like secure login or shopping cart functionality. Without these, the site simply wouldn't work as intended. Performance or analytics cookies collect anonymous data on how visitors use a website, helping site owners understand user behavior and improve performance. Functional cookies remember user choices to provide personalized features, such as remembering your username or region. Lastly, advertising or targeting cookies track browsing habits to deliver more relevant advertisements. Each category serves a distinct role in the complex ecosystem of web functionality and online advertising, contributing to both the user's experience and the website's operational insights. Understanding these distinctions empowers users to make informed decisions about their cookie preferences and privacy settings.

It is also important to differentiate between first-party and third-party cookies. First-party cookies are set by the website you are visiting ‚Äì in our case, YumCraz. They are generally used to store your preferences, remember your login status, or track your activity on our site for internal analytics. Third-party cookies, however, are set by a domain other than the one you are visiting. These are often used for cross-site tracking, advertising, and social media integration. For example, if a YumCraz page includes an embedded video from YouTube, YouTube might set its own cookies on your device. Similarly, if we use an external advertising service, that service might place a third-party cookie to track your interests across different websites. The distinction is crucial because third-party cookies are often the primary concern for privacy-conscious users due to their potential for extensive tracking across the internet. Managing these cookies effectively is key to controlling your online footprint.

We believe in empowering our users to make informed decisions about their privacy, and we provide various tools to help you manage your cookie settings effectively.

Here are several ways you can manage your cookie preferences:

  • **Browser Settings:** You can typically find cookie settings in your browser's "Options" or "Preferences" menu. Each browser handles cookie management slightly differently.
    • **Google Chrome:** Go to Settings > Privacy and security > Site Settings > Cookies and site data. Here you can block third-party cookies, clear cookies, and manage specific site permissions.
    • **Mozilla Firefox:** Go to Options > Privacy & Security > Cookies and Site Data. You can choose to block all cookies, block third-party cookies, or clear data when Firefox closes.
    • **Microsoft Edge:** Go to Settings > Privacy, search, and services > Clear browsing data or Cookies and site permissions. You can manage cookies, block third-party cookies, and set specific site permissions.
    • **Safari (macOS/iOS):** Go to Preferences > Privacy. You can block all cookies, prevent cross-site tracking, and manage website data.
    • **Other Browsers:** Consult your browser's help documentation for instructions on managing cookie settings.
    Please remember that if you delete your cookies, you may need to re-enter your login details or preferences on websites you visit, including YumCraz.
  • **Cookie Consent Tool (on YumCraz):** We provide a dedicated cookie consent dialogue box at the bottom of our website. This tool allows you to accept or decline our use of non-essential cookies. Your preference will be stored, and you can change your mind at any time by revisiting this Cookie Policy page or clearing your browser's cookies. This tool is designed to give you granular control over the types of cookies we use, allowing you to tailor your experience to your privacy comfort level while still enjoying the core functionalities of YumCraz.
  • **Opt-out of Advertising Networks:** Many advertising networks offer you the option to opt out of targeted advertising. You can visit websites like the Network Advertising Initiative (www.networkadvertising.org) or the Digital Advertising Alliance (www.aboutads.info) to learn more about how to opt out of interest-based advertising from participating companies. Opting out of an ad network does not mean you will no longer receive advertising; it means that the advertising you see will not be targeted based on your web browsing activities.
  • **Mobile Device Settings:** For mobile applications, your device settings may offer options to limit ad tracking or reset advertising identifiers. Consult your device manufacturer's instructions for details.

It's important to understand that while you can manage your cookie preferences, disabling certain types of cookies (especially essential cookies) may impact the functionality and performance of the YumCraz website. We strive to provide a balance between a rich, personalized user experience and respecting your privacy choices. Your ability to control cookies is a fundamental aspect of online privacy, and we encourage you to explore these options to ensure your browsing experience aligns with your preferences.
